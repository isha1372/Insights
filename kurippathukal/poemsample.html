A poem for the quiet hour

Nida Rahman Poet & Contributing Writer
June 14, 2026 2 min read

Between Maghrib and Isha there is a door the sky leaves open — a blue not quite night, a light not quite gone.

I have learned to sit in it. Not to fill it with words or plans or the small urgency of everything that didn't get done.

Just to sit. The way a cup sits empty before tea is poured — ready, not lacking.

· · ·

മഗ്‌രിബിനും ഇശായ്ക്കും ഇടയിൽ ആകാശം ഒരു വാതിൽ തുറന്നിടുന്നു — പൂർണ്ണരാത്രിയല്ലാത്ത നീലിമ, കൊഴിഞ്ഞുപോകാത്ത വെളിച്ചം.

That door closes quietly. You won't hear it. But by Isha you will notice you are somehow more yourself.
