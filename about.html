About Gazva Insight

What we publish, who writes for us, and the standard we hold ourselves to.

Gazva Insight is the official publication platform of the Gazva Students' Union of Fathima Zahra Islamic Women's College, Chemmad. Dedicated to knowledge, faith, and creativity, it features articles on the Quran, Fiqh, Islamic history, contemporary issues, and student reflections, alongside stories, poetry, and other creative works.

Vision

To nurture a generation of thoughtful Muslim researchers and writers who engage with revelation, tradition, and contemporary realities through authentic scholarship, intellectual curiosity, and responsible expression.

Mission

Our platform is dedicated to cultivating a vibrant culture of reading, research, and writing among students by providing a space for meaningful academic engagement across diverse fields of Islamic knowledge.

Through Tadabbur, we encourage deep reflection on the Qur'an and its timeless guidance. Ahkaam explores Islamic jurisprudence and its relevance to everyday life. Global Affairs examines contemporary social, political, and humanitarian issues through an informed Islamic perspective. Siyar reconnects readers with the lives, legacy, and historical experiences that have shaped the Muslim world. Reflections offers space for thoughtful essays, personal insights, and critical engagement with ideas, while Bookshelf promotes a culture of reading through reviews and discussions of classical and contemporary works. Through Meet the Scholar, we bring readers closer to scholars, researchers, and intellectuals whose experiences and insights inspire learning and meaningful dialogue.

Together, these spaces aim to foster authentic scholarship, encourage critical thinking, strengthen academic writing, and contribute to a growing repository of beneficial knowledge for students and the wider community.

Our editorial standard

Every article is reviewed before publishing, with sources noted where relevant. When scholars differ on a question, we try to represent that difference honestly rather than flattening it into a single answer.
